Mutual funds is a safe way for those who do not want to take the risk of volatility of stock or the debt market. But, the benefits of wealth creation can be availed with the help of our experts at no extra cost.

Types

Capital Gain Bonds

1.Interest Payable, Rates, Periodicity: Capital gain bonds can be presently purchased for a maximum amount of ₹50 lakhs in denomination of ₹10,000 mainly to save tax on profit made by sale of an asset. Infrastructure Bonds

Currently investment upto ₹20,000 in infrastructure bonds is available for additional deduction of income tax rebate over and above the limit of ₹1 lakh. This deduction is available under section 80CCF of the Income Tax Act. These bonds are generally having 10 years period. The interest rate varies from 7.50% to 8.50% p.a. There is a option for exit after 5 years. The interest payment is on annual basis or on maturity.
