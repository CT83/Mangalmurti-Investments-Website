Saving Schemes

1. National Savings Certificate (VIII issue)

1.Interest Payable, Rates, Periodicity: 8% Interest compounded six monthly but payable at maturity. ₹100 Grows to ₹160.10 after 6 years.
2.Investment Limits & Denominations: Minimum ₹100 No maximum limit available in denominations of ₹100, ₹500, ₹1000, ₹5000 & ₹10,000.
3.Salient Features: A single holder type certificate can be purchased by an adult for himself or on behalf of a minor or to a minor. Deposits quality for tax rebate under Sec. 80C of IT Act. The interest accruing annually but deemed to be reinvested will also qualify for deduction under Section 80C of IT Act. Deposits and interest qualify for income tax relief. Availability of loan from banks.


2. Senior Citizens Savings Scheme

1.Interest Payable, Rates, Periodicity: 9% per annum, payable from the date of deposit of 31st March, 30th September & 31st December in the first instance. Thereafter, interest shall be payable on 31st March, 30th June, 30th September and 31st December.
2.Investment Limits & Denominations: There shall be only one deposit in the account in multiple of ₹1000 to ₹15,00,000.
3.Salient Features: Maturity period is 5 years. A depositor may operate more than a account in individual capacity or jointly with spouse. Age should be 60 years or more, and 55 years or more but less than 60 years who has retired on superannuation or otherwise on the date of opening of account subject to the condition that the account is opened within one month of receipt of retirement benefits. Premature closure is allowed after one year on deduction of 1.5% interest & after 2 years 1% interest. TDS is deducted at source on interest if the interest amount is more than ₹10,000 p.a. The investment under this scheme qualify for the benefit of Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961 from 1.4.2007.
3.Sec 80C Benefit: Investments up to ₹1,00,000 in specified securities (Maximum of ₹70,000 in PPF) qualify for deduction.
